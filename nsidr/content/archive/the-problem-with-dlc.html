--- title: "The problem with DLC" date: 1970-01-01T00:00:00Z contributors: - Cory Faller --- The whole on-disc-or-not thing, the developed-after-game or not, these are discussion points sure but they are not the CORE issue

The core issue is that we don't know how to assign monetary value to "part of a game"

We've never had to before. You buy the game and it costs how much it costs cause that's what all games cost. Sometimes it could seem "pretty light" and maybe it's a little cheaper then, but we never assigned value to specific PARTS.

Well that's what we're doing now. How much is a level worth? How much is a character worth? Back in the day, a game could have 8 fighters or 30 fighters and that was never reflected in the price. Why now?

Is it just project scope? Who scopes the project? How are we as consumers supposed to TRUST that it's a genuine scope thing, and not a money-gouging attempt?
