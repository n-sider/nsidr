--- title: "Capcom shares soar on Wii games" date: 2007-09-12T13:07:54Z contributors: - Mattie Behrens --- Capcom is set to increase their Wii commitment by doubling the number of titles they have headed for the system to six, noting they expect sales to go up 11% for the year as they do so—and they're seeing record stock prices on expectations they'll be succeeding with the platform.

The benchmark so far is Resident Evil 4: Wii Edition, which is expected to beat Capcom's original ship estimates. On the horizon, Capcom has Resident Evil: Umbrella Chronicles—intended for use with the Wii Zapper, which Nintendo will start selling November 19—and the lesser-known Zack & Wiki: Quest for Barbaros' Treasure. New titles have yet to be announced, but we're waiting with bated breath.
