Your fanbase is reduced to taking photos of your booth and running them through Photoshop until things start appearing.

Original image by IGN; enhanced by NeoGAF's HAL_Laboratory

Consensus is that underneath the sheet is a NES-era-styled logo for a new game called "Super Mario Bros. Wii". The last game we had that looked like this was the DS' New Super Mario Bros., a 2.5D outing that was pretty tremendously well-received sales-wise, though not as universally loved by the fans as its predecessors Super Mario Bros. 3 or Super Mario World (and if you think I'm going to get into which of those is better, you're crazy.) It's conceivable, at least, that this game—if it in fact exists and we're not all being played for fools—could follow in those footsteps.

The existence of some sort of Wii Mario game fits with Nikkei's earlier report that Nintendo would bring such an animal to try to boost Wii's fortunes in Japan and to maintain momentum elsewhere. And beyond this rampant speculation and photoshoppery, we also do know that Nintendo has Sin & Punishment 2, Final Fantasy Crystal Chronicles: The Crystal Bearers and Kingdom Hearts: 358/2 Days in their booth as well.
