--- title: "New Japanese releases dominate sales charts" date: 2006-11-30T01:31:53Z contributors: - Brandon Daiker --- The numbers for the week of November 20-26 are freshly in from Japan, and everything's coming up Milhouse:

  1. DS (Sega) - Love and Berry DS - 421,227 / NEW
  2. DS (Nintendo) - Jump Ultimate Stars - 150,303 / NEW
  3. PS2 (Konami) - J-League Winning Eleven 10 + Europe League 06-07 - 103,365 / NEW
  4. DS (Nintendo) - Common Knowledge Training - 81813 / 611,642
  5. DS (Nintendo) - Pokemon Diamond - 76183 / 1,827,226
  6. DS (Nintendo) - Pokemon Pearl - 58158 / 1,539,775
  7. DS (Nintendo) - Hoshi no Kirby - 55824 / 352,082
  8. Xbox360 (Tecmo) - Dead or Alive Xtreme 2 - 45536 / NEW
  9. PSP (Sony) - Jeanne D'Arc PSP - 45387 / NEW
  10. DS (Nintendo) - New Super Mario Bros. - 35086 / 3,451,994

Topping the charts are Love and Berry, a ridiculous card-swiping game that found success in Japanese arcades with little girls who love to collect things, and Jump Ultimate Stars, the sequel to another DS game that featured all sorts of Japanese manga characters you've never heard of.

Of particular note is the fact that no PS3 titles sit on the chart. We suspect that this is because unlike XBox 360 charter Dead or Alive Xtreme 2, giant enemy crabs do not have equally giant breasts.
